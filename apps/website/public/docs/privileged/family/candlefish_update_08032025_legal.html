Executive Summary

This memorandum proposes a studio structure for Candlefish.ai Studio that:

  • Maintains compliance with SEC Rule 204A-11 and FINRA Rule 32102
  • Provides for orderly transition of ownership upon change in employment status
  • Establishes clear governance and operational boundaries
  • Protects family interests while maintaining business flexibility

Current Status and Regulatory Context

Candlefish AI is currently pre-revenue with active business development efforts underway. Due to Karen Smith's and my continued employment at Morgan Stanley, we are subject to regulatory restrictions that require careful structuring of any outside business activities.

Specifically, FINRA Rule 3210 requires prior written notice to the member firm before engaging in outside business activities, and SEC regulations impose restrictions on securities-related activities by associated persons of investment advisers. To maintain full compliance, I currently hold 100% ownership of Candlefish.ai Holdings LLC.

During MS employment period (through ~2027):

Delaware vs Colorado LLC Analysis

Current: Colorado LLC (During MS Employment)

  • Simpler compliance and reporting requirements
  • Lower formation and maintenance costs
  • Adequate for single-member LLC structure
  • Local registered agent readily available

Future: Delaware LLC (Post-MS Employment, ~2027)

Why Delaware conversion makes sense post-MS:

  • Superior business law jurisprudence and Court of Chancery
  • More flexible operating agreement provisions
  • Better for multi-member LLC with complex profit sharing
  • Preferred by institutional investors and acquirers
  • Enhanced privacy protections for members
  • Series LLC option for subsidiary structure

Timing: Conversion to Delaware LLC should occur after MS employment ends to avoid additional compliance complexity during employment period.
