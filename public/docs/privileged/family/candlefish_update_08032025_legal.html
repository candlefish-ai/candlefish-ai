Executive Summary

This memorandum proposes a corporate structure for Candlefish AI that:

  • Maintains compliance with SEC Rule 204A-11 and FINRA Rule 32102
  • Provides for orderly transition of ownership upon change in employment status
  • Establishes clear governance and operational boundaries
  • Protects family interests while maintaining business flexibility

Current Status and Regulatory Context

Candlefish AI is currently pre-revenue with active business development efforts underway. Due to Karen Smith's and my continued employment at Morgan Stanley, we are subject to regulatory restrictions that require careful structuring of any outside business activities.

Specifically, FINRA Rule 3210 requires prior written notice to the member firm before engaging in outside business activities, and SEC regulations impose restrictions on securities-related activities by associated persons of investment advisers. To maintain full compliance, I currently hold 100% ownership of Candlefish Holdings LLC.

Proposed Ownership Structure

The ownership structure is designed to transition based on employment status:

Phase 1: During Morgan Stanley Employment

  • Patrick Smith: 100% ownership of Candlefish Holdings LLC
  • Structure: Single-member LLC for simplicity and compliance
  • Rationale: Maintains clear compliance boundaries

Phase 2: Post-Employment Transition

Upon both Karen's and my departure from Morgan Stanley (timing hypothetical and not predetermined):

  • Karen and Patrick Smith: Equal majority control (combined 70-80%)
  • Tyler, Kendall, and Trevor Smith: Remaining equity (20-30% divided equally)
  • Implementation: Operating Agreement amendment triggered by employment status change

Corporate Structure

Parent Entity: Candlefish Holdings LLC

  • Houses all intellectual property, trademarks, and core technology
  • Delaware LLC for favorable business law framework
  • Pass-through taxation via Schedule K-1 distributions

Subsidiary Entities

Each vertical market operates through a separate subsidiary LLC:

  • Candlefish Trophy LLC
  • Candlefish Paint LLC
  • Candlefish Brewery LLC
  • Additional subsidiaries as markets develop

This structure provides:

  • Legal liability isolation between business units
  • Clear financial reporting and performance tracking
  • Flexibility for unit-specific partnerships or exits

Governance Framework

Management Structure

  • CEO/Managing Member: Day-to-day operational decisions
  • Annual Family Board Meeting: Major strategic decisions
  • Decisions requiring family approval:
    • Launch of new subsidiaries
    • Sale of equity exceeding 20% in any entity
    • Compensation structure modifications
    • Full or partial exit transactions

Employment and Compensation

Active Participation

  • Tyler Smith: Initial employee with market-rate base salary plus performance incentives
  • Other family members: May join as employees or consultants at market rates
  • Compensation methodology: Third-party benchmarking to ensure arm's length terms

Passive Ownership

  • Profit distributions based on ownership percentages
  • No employment requirement for ownership retention
  • Anti-dilution provisions for family members

Financial Structure

  • Revenue flows to operational subsidiaries
  • Management fee (15-20%) paid to parent company for shared services
  • Annual profit distributions after reserve requirements
  • No cross-subsidization between subsidiaries

Risk Management

  • Key person life insurance on Patrick Smith
  • Buy-sell agreement funded by insurance proceeds
  • Annual third-party CPA review
  • Annual legal compliance review
  • Quarterly board reporting requirements

Exit Strategies

The structure accommodates multiple exit scenarios:

  • Individual subsidiary sales with parent company royalty/carry
  • Strategic minority investments (capped at 20% per subsidiary)
  • Full portfolio acquisition
  • Family buyout provisions

Recommendations for Implementation

  1. Obtain formal written approval from Morgan Stanley compliance for current structure
  2. Draft comprehensive Operating Agreement with phase-based ownership provisions
  3. Establish subsidiary formation templates for rapid market entry
  4. Implement governance calendar and reporting frameworks
  5. Consider establishing advisory board with independent directors
