Confidential Family Business Structure Document

Version A: Legal Compliance Review
Date: August 3, 2025

Preliminary Business Context

This document outlines the proposed business structure for Candlefish, with full acknowledgment of its pre-revenue status and contingent future development.

Regulatory Compliance Preface

Due to specific regulatory constraints imposed by Morgan Stanley's employee conduct policies[1], the following structural provisions are designed to ensure strict compliance with SEC Rule 204A-1 and FINRA Rule 3210[2]:

  1. 100% Initial Ownership Structure: To mitigate potential conflicts of interest, a single-member ownership model will be initially implemented.
  2. Future Ownership Conversion: Upon formal separation from Morgan Stanley, a pro-rata equity redistribution is contemplated.

Business Entity Architecture

Holding Company Structure

Candlefish Holdings LLC will serve as the parent company, implementing a structured subsidiary model to:

  • Isolate individual business vertical risks
  • Maintain clear corporate governance
  • Provide flexible strategic adaptation

Subsidiary Operational Framework

Each industry vertical will operate as a distinct subsidiary, with the following key provisions:

  • Independent financial accountability
  • Discrete revenue and cost centers
  • Limited external investment (maximum 20% non-voting equity)

Compensation and Equity Allocation

Family Member Participation

Compensation and participation will be structured to ensure:

  • Market-rate base compensation for active roles
  • Performance-based incentive structures
  • Equitable long-term value preservation

Profit Distribution Mechanism

Annual distributions will occur via K-1 tax reporting, with strict guidelines:

  • Subsidiary revenue covers direct operational costs
  • Fixed percentage allocated to parent company for shared infrastructure
  • No cross-subsidization between business units

Governance and Decision-Making

Organizational Oversight

Governance will maintain a structured yet flexible approach:

  • Annual family review of strategic initiatives
  • Quarterly transparent reporting
  • External professional review (CPA and legal counsel)

Exit and Transition Provisions

Multiple potential exit strategies are contemplated:

  • Individual subsidiary divestment
  • Comprehensive portfolio transaction
  • Key-person insurance and buy-sell agreement provisions

[1] References: SEC Rule 204A-1 (Code of Ethics for Investment Advisers), FINRA Rule 3210 (Outside Business Activities of Registered Persons)

[2] This document represents a proposed structure subject to final legal and compliance review. Not a binding agreement.
